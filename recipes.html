Classic German Sauerkraut

A simple, traditional recipe that yields tangy, probiotic-rich sauerkraut. Just two ingredients — cabbage and salt — transformed by the magic of lacto-fermentation.

Prep: 30 min
Ferment: 14 days
Easy

Ingredients

  • 1 medium head green cabbage (about 2 lbs)
  • 1 tablespoon sea salt (non-iodized)
  • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds (optional)
  • 1 wide-mouth mason jar with fermentation lid

Interactive Diagnosis

The Fermentation Trouble-O-Meter

Is your ferment safe, or did something go wrong? Check the symptoms below to diagnose your jar immediately.

White Surface Film

Harmless

A thin, white, wrinkly film on top of your liquid is usually Kahm yeast. It is harmless and caused by contact with oxygen. Simply skim it off and keep fermenting.

Cloudy Brine

Caution

Brine turning cloudy is a completely normal sign of active lactic acid bacterial growth! As long as there is no rotten smell, cloudy brine means your microbes are working hard.

Fuzzy Colored Mold

Discard

If you see fuzzy, colored (green, blue, black, or pink) mold patches on top of vegetables, the batch has been compromised. Discard, sterilize your jar, and start again.

Pro Tips

Fermentation Tips from the Experts

Temperature Matters

Ferment at 65–75°F (18–24°C). Cooler = slower but better flavor. Warmer = faster but mushy texture.

Keep It Submerged

Vegetables must stay below the brine. Use fermentation weights or a zip-lock bag.

Weigh Your Salt

Always use a kitchen scale. 2% salt by weight of vegetables is the golden ratio for most ferments.
