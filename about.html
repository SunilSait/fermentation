Our Journey

How We Cultivated Our History

Fermentation is about patience and slow development. So is our history. Explore the key milestones of our craft.

2014

The Farmhouse Spark

Founder Sarah Jenkins began teaching traditional sourdough and cabbage lacto-fermentation workshops right out of her farmhouse kitchen homestead, sharing sourdough starter cultures with eager neighbors.

2017

Artisan Workshop Launch

Local classes rapidly expanded into our first dedicated physical classroom. We developed structured curriculum modules for authentic Kimchi, Sauerkraut, and SCOBY kombucha care.

2020

Going Global & Digital

Responding to global shutdowns, we launched live virtual broadcasts and packaged physical Fermentation Supply Kits to ship starter cultures safely to kitchens around the world.

2023

Storefront & Curated Supplies

Opened our physical storefront and curated online supply catalog, sourcing premium non-iodized Celtic sea salts, hand-blown weight stones, glass containers, and airlock lids.

Present Day

5,000+ Graduated Fermenters

Today, we support a massive global family of fermenters logging pH levels, sharing recipes, and pickling together to foster healthy, probiotic-rich kitchen lifestyles.

Our Philosophy

Clean Ingredients, Ancient Biology

Artisanal Integrity

We source organic ingredients, heirloom mother cultures, and hand-harvested minerals to ensure that your fermenting projects contain only pure, uncompromised, and healthy elements.

Scientific Precision

Fermentation is a living science. We teach pH-level tracking, temperature management, and strict anaerobic practices so that your ferments remain safe, active, and delicious.

Community Centered

We partner with local farms, share student-contributed recipes, and foster open forums. We believe pickling is an ancient tradition best experienced when shared.
